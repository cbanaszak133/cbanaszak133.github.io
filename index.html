Bio

I am a former high school Physics and Computer Science teacher looking to make a change into the software development field. I have greatly enjoyed my time as an educator and am looking forward to the new challenges that software engineering will give me.

Due to my prior experiences, I believe I can bring a diverse range of skills to the software field. I was originally a Physics major at Loyola University Chicago where I used quantitative programming tools such as Mathematica and LabView in order to model complex physical phenomenon. My major in general has also given me great critical thinking skills that will aid me in formulating solutions to complex problems.

My teaching experience has given me a useful range of skills, particularly in the social realm. Being an educator allowed me to develop my public speaking skills, which will be incredibly important to relay information to others in a clear and concise manner. Teaching computer science courses in object oriented programming has also introduced me to interesting progamming technqiues and practices.
